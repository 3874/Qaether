Soft Flux Energy, Defect 2-Chains, and a Linear Potential from Spacetime Worldsheets

Abstract
We formulate a confinement lower bound in Qaether theory, a discrete gauge-like model on a 3-complex built from tetrahedra and octahedra, with $\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ link variables. From an oriented link 1-cochain $k\in C^1(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12})$, we define plaquette flux (discrete curvature) $Q=\delta k\in C^2(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12})$. Replacing a hard constraint $Q=0$ by a soft flux energy $\epsilon_x(Q_x)$ satisfying $\epsilon_x(0)=0$ and $\epsilon_x(Q\neq0)\ge\epsilon_{\min}>0$, we obtain a universal energy lower bound proportional to the number of nonzero-flux plaquettes. The key topological input is that defects cannot terminate arbitrarily in the bulk: not merely because $\partial\partial=0$ always holds, but because the field-derived relation $Q=\delta k$ forces the discrete Bianchi identity $\delta Q=\delta^2 k=0$. Identifying cochains and chains via the oriented plaquette basis, this becomes $\partial S_Q=0$ for the corresponding defect 2-chain $S_Q$, implying closed defect shells in the source-free sector. In spacetime $\mathcal{X}=X\times{0,1,\dots,T}$, inserting a static quark–antiquark pair of $\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ charge $\pm q$ is axiomatized by the existence of an open defect worldsheet $\Sigma\in C_2(\mathcal{X};\mathbb{Z}_{12})$ with $\partial \Sigma=W_{R,T}$, where $W_{R,T}\in C_1(\mathcal{X};\mathbb{Z}_{12})$ is the oriented sum of worldlines. A slice-by-slice cut argument yields $|\mathrm{supp}(\Sigma)|\ge cRT$, hence $E(R,T)\ge \sigma RT$ and the static potential $V(R)=\lim_{T\to\infty}E(R,T)/T\ge \sigma R$ with $\sigma=\epsilon_{\min}c>0$. We also bound the minimal nucleation energy for a nontrivial closed defect shell, leading to a “nucleation gap + area term” bound $E(R,T)\ge E_{\mathrm{break}}+\sigma RT$. While the constant $E_{\mathrm{break}}$ vanishes in the $T\to\infty$ extraction of $V(R)$, it quantifies vacuum stability and the minimal cost to create a defect shell.

1. Introduction

Confinement in non-abelian gauge theory is traditionally diagnosed by the Wilson-loop area law and a linearly rising static potential. In lattice formulations, strong-coupling expansions naturally produce surfaces spanning Wilson loops, suggesting a geometric route to confinement. Qaether theory is a discrete model with $\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ link variables defined on a mixed tetrahedral–octahedral 3-complex, motivated as an infrared effective description in which flux defects carry energy.

This paper isolates a minimal set of assumptions under which a rigorous lower bound implying linear confinement follows:

  1. Soft flux gap: any nonzero plaquette flux has energy at least $\epsilon_{\min}>0$.
  2. Topological constraint from field-derived curvature: $Q=\delta k$ implies $\delta Q=0$ (discrete Bianchi), so defects are closed in the absence of sources.
  3. Source insertion forces an open worldsheet: external static charges are represented by worldlines $W$, and defects must form a worldsheet $\Sigma$ with $\partial\Sigma=W$.
  4. Geometric lower bound on spanning area: any $\Sigma$ bounding two separated worldlines has $|\mathrm{supp}(\Sigma)|\gtrsim RT$.

From these, the linear potential lower bound follows.

Important conceptual point. The statement “defects cannot end in the bulk” does not follow from $\partial\partial=0$ alone (which is a tautology for all chains). It follows because $Q$ is not an arbitrary 2-chain but a curvature cochain derived from a link cochain, $Q=\delta k$, which forces $\delta Q=0$.

2. Lattice 3-Complex and $\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ Link Variables

Axiom 2.1 (3-complex)
Space is an oriented finite 3-complex
$$
X=(V,E,P,C_3),\qquad P=P_3\cup P_4,
$$
where $P_3$ is the set of triangular plaquettes and $P_4$ the set of square plaquettes. Each 3-cell in $C_3$ is either a tetrahedron or an octahedron (as in an FCC-type mixed decomposition).

Axiom 2.2 ($\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ link variables)
To each oriented edge $e=(i\to j)\in E$ assign
$$
k(e)=k_{ij}\in\mathbb{Z}_{12},\qquad k_{ji}\equiv -k_{ij}\ (\mathrm{mod}\ 12).
$$
Interpret the phase difference as $\Delta\phi_{ij}=\frac{\pi}{6}k_{ij}\ (\mathrm{mod}\ 2\pi)$.

Definition 2.3 (Plaquette flux / discrete curvature)
Let $k\in C^1(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12})$. Define the plaquette flux cochain
$$
\boxed{Q:=\delta k\in C^2(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12})}.
$$
For each oriented plaquette $x\in P$,
$$
\boxed{Q_x=\langle Q,x\rangle=\sum_{e\in\partial x}\mathrm{sgn}(x,e)k(e)\ (\mathrm{mod}\ 12)}.
$$
$Q_x=0$ is the flux-free (“closed”) state.

3. Soft Flux Hamiltonian

Axiom 3.1 (Soft flux energy)
For each plaquette $x\in P$, choose $\epsilon_x:\mathbb{Z}_{12}\to\mathbb{R}_{\ge0}$ such that:

  1. $\epsilon_x(0)=0$,
  2. $Q\neq0 \Rightarrow \epsilon_x(Q)\ge \epsilon_{\min}>0$,
  3. $\epsilon_x(Q)=\epsilon_x(12-Q)$ (charge conjugation symmetry, optional but natural).

Define
$$
\boxed{\epsilon_{\min}:=\min_{x\in P}\min_{Q\neq0}\epsilon_x(Q) > 0.}
$$

Definition 3.2 (Soft flux Hamiltonian)
$$
\boxed{H_{\mathrm{flux}}^{\mathrm{soft}}(k)=\sum_{x\in P}\epsilon_x\bigl(Q_x(k)\bigr)}.
$$
_Example:_ $\epsilon(Q)=\mu\bigl(1-\cos(2\pi Q/12)\bigr)$ yields $\epsilon_{\min}=\mu(1-\cos(\pi/6))>0$.

4. Defect 2-Chains and the Discrete Bianchi Identity

4.1 Chain–cochain identification (basis-level)
Fix the oriented plaquette basis of $C_2(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12})$ and the dual basis of $C^2(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12})$. This gives a canonical coefficient-wise identification:
$$
C^2(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12})\ \cong\ C_2(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12}),
$$
sending a 2-cochain $Q$ to the 2-chain with the same coefficients in the plaquette basis.

Definition 4.1 (Defect 2-chain)
Let $Q=\delta k$. Define the defect 2-chain
$$
\boxed{S_Q:=\sum_{x\in P} Q_x x\ \in C_2(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12}).}
$$
Define the defect support and defect “area”:
$$
\mathrm{supp}(S_Q):={x\in P: Q_x\neq0},\qquad |S_Q|:=|\mathrm{supp}(S_Q)|.
$$

Theorem 4.2 (Discrete Bianchi identity)
Since $Q=\delta k$,
$$
\boxed{\delta Q=\delta^2 k=0\in C^3(X;\mathbb{Z}_{12}).}
$$
Equivalently, for every 3-cell $c\in C_3$,
$$
\langle \delta Q,c\rangle=\langle Q,\partial c\rangle=\sum_{x\subset\partial c}\mathrm{sgn}(c,x)Q_x=0.
$$

Corollary 4.3 (Source-free closure of defects)
Under the identification above, $\delta Q=0$ implies
$$
\boxed{\partial S_Q=0.}
$$
Hence, in the absence of external sources, defects form closed shells (no bulk endpoints).

_Why this matters:_ $\partial\partial=0$ is always true for any chain and does not constrain where a defect could end. The constraint arises only because the defect is curvature derived from a link field: $Q=\delta k\Rightarrow \delta Q=0$.

5. Universal Energy Lower Bound from Defect Area

Theorem 5.1 (Energy bound)
$$
\boxed{H_{\mathrm{flux}}^{\mathrm{soft}}(k)\ \ge\ \epsilon_{\min} |S_Q|.}
$$

Proof. For each $x\in\mathrm{supp}(S_Q)$, $Q_x\neq0$, hence $\epsilon_x(Q_x)\ge \epsilon_{\min}$. Summing over plaquettes gives the bound. $\square$

6. Source-Free Sector: Minimal Closed Shells and Nucleation Gap

To create defects in vacuum, one must nucleate a nontrivial closed 2-cycle/shell.

Assumption 6.1 (No smaller closed shells than cell boundaries)
We assume the minimal nontrivial closed defect shell in the triangular sector is realised by the boundary of a single 3-cell (a standard “local minimal sphere” assumption for this mixed complex). Concretely:

  • Generic sector: the boundary of a tetrahedron (4 triangles) is allowed.
  • Octahedral-only sector (optional strengthening): tetrahedral shells are forbidden/suppressed, and the minimal allowed shell is the boundary of an octahedron (8 triangles).

This assumption can be replaced by a purely topological constant $N_{\min}$ defined as “the minimal number of triangular plaquettes in any nonzero closed 2-chain allowed in the sector.”

Lemma 6.2 (Minimal shell size)
Let $N_{\min}$ denote the minimal number of plaquettes in any allowed nonzero closed defect shell. Under Assumption 6.1:
$$
\boxed{N_{\min}=4\ \ (\text{generic}),\qquad N_{\min}=8\ \ (\text{octahedral-only}).}
$$

Corollary 6.3 (Nucleation energy gap)
Any nontrivial closed defect shell costs at least
$$
\boxed{E_{\mathrm{break}}\ \ge\ N_{\min}\epsilon_{\min}}
$$
i.e., $E_{\mathrm{break}}\ge 4\epsilon_{\min}$ (generic) or $E_{\mathrm{break}}\ge 8\epsilon_{\min}$ (octahedral-only).

7. Spacetime Formulation and External Static Sources

Definition 7.1 (Spacetime complex)
Let
$$
\boxed{\mathcal{X}:=X\times{0,1,\dots,T}}
$$
be the discrete spacetime complex. Cochains/chains on $\mathcal{X}$ are defined similarly.

Definition 7.2 (Static sources as $\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ worldlines)
Fix a charge $q\in\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ (often $q=1$). A static quark–antiquark pair separated by spatial distance $R$ is represented by an oriented 1-chain
$$
\boxed{W_{R,T}\in C_1(\mathcal{X};\mathbb{Z}_{12})}
$$
given by the sum of two time-directed worldlines with coefficients $+q$ and $-q$ (and opposite orientations as appropriate). This ensures $\partial W_{R,T}=0$ for periodic time boundary conditions, or gives boundary insertions at the time ends in an open-time cylinder formulation.

Axiom 7.3 (Source forcing: open defect worldsheet)
In the presence of the external source $W_{R,T}$, admissible configurations include a defect worldsheet
$$
\boxed{\Sigma\in C_2(\mathcal{X};\mathbb{Z}_{12})\quad \text{such that}\quad \partial \Sigma=W_{R,T}.}
$$
$\Sigma$ is interpreted as the set (with $\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ coefficients) of plaquettes carrying nonzero flux that must span the source worldlines.

This axiom is the discrete analogue of the strong-coupling/Wilson-loop “surface spanning the loop” structure: the source cannot be realised without an attached flux sheet whose boundary is the source.

8. Cut Argument: Lower Bound on Spanning Worldsheet Area

We now bound the minimal number of plaquettes needed for any $\Sigma$ with $\partial\Sigma=W_{R,T}$.

Lemma 8.1 (Slice-by-slice cut bound)
There exists a lattice-dependent constant $c>0$ such that for any $\Sigma\in C_2(\mathcal{X};\mathbb{Z}_{12})$ with $\partial\Sigma=W_{R,T}$,
$$
\boxed{|\Sigma|\ \ge\ c R T,}
$$
where $|\Sigma|:=|\mathrm{supp}(\Sigma)|$ counts plaquettes with nonzero coefficient.

Proof (standard cut argument, with explicit constant).
For each time slice $t$, intersect $\Sigma$ with the slice. The boundary condition $\partial\Sigma=W_{R,T}$ forces the slice intersection to include a “cut” that connects the two source positions, hence requires at least $\ge R$ units of spatial separation measure (graph distance). Each plaquette can contribute to the intersection of only a uniformly bounded number $\eta<\infty$ of slices/cut units (a local combinatorial bound depending on maximal incidence/degree and allowed orientations). Therefore the total number of plaquettes must satisfy
$$
|\Sigma|\ \ge\ \frac{1}{\eta}RT.
$$
Set $c:=1/\eta$. $\square$

9. Main Confinement Theorem

Theorem 9.1 (Area-law lower bound and string tension)
Let $Q=\delta k$ on $\mathcal{X}$, let $\Sigma$ be a defect worldsheet satisfying $\partial\Sigma=W_{R,T}$, and let $H_{\mathrm{flux}}^{\mathrm{soft}}$ be the soft flux Hamiltonian. Then
$$
\boxed{E(R,T)\ \ge\ H_{\mathrm{flux}}^{\mathrm{soft}}(k)\ \ge\ \epsilon_{\min}|\Sigma|\ \ge\ \epsilon_{\min}cRT.}
$$
Define the string tension
$$
\boxed{\sigma:=\epsilon_{\min}c\ >0.}
$$
Then
$$
\boxed{E(R,T)\ \ge\ \sigma RT.}
$$

Corollary 9.2 (Linear confinement potential)
Define the static potential by
$$
V(R):=\lim_{T\to\infty}\frac{E(R,T)}{T}.
$$
Then
$$
\boxed{V(R)\ \ge\ \sigma R,}
$$
i.e., the potential grows at least linearly with separation: confinement.

10. Nucleation Gap + Area Term (“Jump + Linear” refined statement)

The source-free nucleation gap quantified by $E_{\mathrm{break}}$ combines with the spanning area cost.

Theorem 10.1 (Nucleation + area lower bound)
Assume the vacuum sector is fully closed (no defects) and that creating defects requires nucleating an allowed closed shell. Then the energy (or Euclidean free energy/action, depending on the implementation of the static source) satisfies
$$
\boxed{E(R,T)\ \ge\ E_{\mathrm{break}}+\sigma RT,}
\qquad E_{\mathrm{break}}\ge N_{\min}\epsilon_{\min}.
$$

Remark (what survives in $V(R)$).
When extracting $V(R)$ via $T\to\infty$,
$$
\frac{E_{\mathrm{break}}}{T}\to 0,
$$
so the linear term determines the asymptotic potential, while $E_{\mathrm{break}}$ captures vacuum stability and the minimum cost to nucleate a defect shell before a long worldsheet can be formed.

11. Discussion and Outlook

What is robust here. The confinement lower bound uses only:

  1. A strictly positive minimal flux cost $\epsilon_{\min}>0$.
  2. The field-derived Bianchi identity $\delta Q=0$ (from $Q=\delta k$).
  3. An axiomatized source coupling that forces an open worldsheet.
  4. A purely combinatorial area lower bound $|\Sigma|\gtrsim RT$.

No detailed functional form of $\epsilon(Q)$ is required beyond a gap at nonzero flux.

Where the model-dependent content lives.

  • The value of $c$ (hence $\sigma$) is geometric/combinatorial and depends on the lattice/complex incidence structure.
  • The minimal shell size $N_{\min}$ depends on which defect shells are dynamically allowed (generic vs octahedral-only sector).
  • A fully dynamical treatment of quark pair creation would modify the long-distance behaviour via string breaking; here we establish a lower bound in the static-source setting.

Future directions.

  • Make Axiom 7.3 derivable from an explicit source coupling term in the Qaether action/Hamiltonian (e.g., a $\mathbb{Z}_{12}$ Wilson insertion).
  • Compute or bound $c$ explicitly for the specific tetrahedral–octahedral complex used in Qaether.
  • Extend to dynamical matter and identify when/if screening weakens the bound.
  • Explore continuum scaling limits and possible emergence of effective string actions.
